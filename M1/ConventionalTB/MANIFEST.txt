Fixes applied (see design.md "Fixes applied" for full rationale):
  DUT (async_fifo2.sv):
    A. Removed dual-driver violation: `fifo_count` is no longer written by
       two `always_ff` blocks on different clocks. `fifo_memory` is now a
       pure-storage module; the half_full / half_empty flags are computed
       per-domain in write_pointer (from binary_wptr - gray-to-bin(wq2_rptr))
       and read_pointer (from gray-to-bin(rq2_wptr) - binary_rptr).
    B. Corrected sync flop reset domains: `sync_w2r` (clocked by rclk) is
       now reset by rrst (was wrst); `sync_r2w` (clocked by wclk) is now
       reset by wrst (was rrst). Each sync now uses its destination
       domain's reset, the canonical Cummings pattern.
    C. Added `rData <= '0` to the read-side reset branch in fifo_memory.
       Previously rData was uninitialized at startup ('X') until the first
       `rinc & ~rEmpty` event.
  TB (async_fifo_tb.sv):
    D. Added a leading comment explaining that the `check_wFull` and
       `check_rEmpty` tasks are intentionally one-shot (because a naive
       continuous monitor would false-error during the sync window) and
       changed `fork ... join` to `fork ... join_none` so the main initial
       block doesn't wait on them. Behaviour is otherwise unchanged.
